# Bylaws of Earth Bridge Alliance

## Article 1 — Name

An association governed by the French Law of 1 July 1901 and the Decree of 16 August 1901 is hereby established under the name:

**Earth Bridge Alliance**

---

## Article 2 — Purpose

The purpose of the Association is to promote sustainable development, environmental stewardship, education, cultural exchange, and community empowerment through international cooperation, with a particular focus on building long-term partnerships between Europe and the Putumayo region of Colombia.

To achieve this purpose, the Association may organize educational, cultural, environmental, humanitarian, scientific, fundraising, and community-based activities, as well as collaborate with public institutions, nonprofit organizations, businesses, and individuals whose objectives align with its mission.

---

## Article 3 — Registered Office

The registered office of the Association shall be located at:

> *[French address]*

The registered office may be transferred by decision of the Board, subject to ratification by the General Assembly where required.

---

## Article 4 — Duration

The Association is established for an unlimited duration.

---

## Article 5 — Membership

Membership is open to any natural or legal person who supports the purpose of the Association and whose application is accepted by the Board.

Members may resign at any time by written notice to the Board.

Membership may be terminated for serious cause by decision of the Board after the member has been given an opportunity to present their observations.

---

## Article 6 — Resources

The resources of the Association may include:

- Membership fees;
- Donations and gifts;
- Public or private grants;
- Sponsorships and partnerships;
- Income generated through activities consistent with the Association's purpose;
- Any other lawful resources permitted under French law.

All income and assets shall be used exclusively to pursue the nonprofit purpose of the Association.

---

## Article 7 — General Assembly

The General Assembly consists of all members of the Association.

It is responsible for:

- approving amendments to these bylaws;
- electing or dismissing members of the Board;
- approving the annual activity and financial reports;
- deciding on the dissolution of the Association;
- any other matters reserved to it by law or these bylaws.

Unless otherwise required by law or these bylaws, decisions are adopted by a simple majority of the votes cast.

---

## Article 8 — Board

The Association shall be administered by a Board composed of at least:

- a President;
- a Treasurer.

The General Assembly may create additional positions as necessary.

The Board is responsible for the day-to-day administration of the Association and the implementation of decisions adopted by the General Assembly.

---

## Article 9 — Representation

The President represents the Association in all civil matters and acts on its behalf before public authorities and third parties.

The President may delegate specific powers to another member of the Board where appropriate.

---

## Article 10 — Amendment of the Bylaws

These bylaws may be amended only by decision of the General Assembly.

Amendments require a two-thirds majority of the votes cast unless a higher majority is required by applicable law.

---

## Article 11 — Dissolution

The Association may be dissolved only by decision of the General Assembly.

Upon dissolution, after settlement of all liabilities, any remaining assets shall be transferred to one or more nonprofit organizations pursuing objectives similar to those of the Association, in accordance with applicable French law.

No part of the Association's assets may be distributed among its members.

---

## Final Provisions

These bylaws were adopted by the founding members on **30 June 2026** and shall enter into force upon the legal registration of the Association.
