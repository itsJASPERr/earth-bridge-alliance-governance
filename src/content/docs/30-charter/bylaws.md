# Bylaws of Earth Bridge Alliance

## Article 1 — Name

The association is named **Earth Bridge Alliance**.

---

## Article 2 — Purpose

The purpose of the Association is to foster sustainable development and cross-cultural exchange by building bridges between Europe and the Putumayo region of Colombia.

Its mission is based on four pillars:

- education
- environmental stewardship
- community empowerment
- cultural resilience

The Association may carry out any lawful activity that directly or indirectly contributes to the achievement of its purpose.

---

## Article 3 — Headquarters

The headquarters of the Association are located at:

**46 boulevard Notre-Dame, 13006 Marseille, France**

The headquarters may be transferred by decision of the Board, subject to ratification by the next General Assembly if required by applicable law.

---

## Article 4 — Duration

The Association is established for an unlimited duration.

---

## Article 5 — Membership

Membership is open to any natural person or legal entity supporting the purpose of the Association.

Admission and exclusion of members are decided by the Board in accordance with objective and non-discriminatory criteria.

Members may resign at any time by written notice addressed to the President.

All members have equal voting rights unless otherwise provided by these Bylaws.

Membership ceases by:

- resignation;
- death or dissolution of the legal entity;
- exclusion decided by the Board for legitimate cause, after the member has been given the opportunity to present their observations.

---

## Article 6 — General Assembly

The General Assembly consists of all members of the Association.

It is responsible for:

- adopting and amending the Bylaws;
- electing and dismissing members of the Board;
- approving annual activity and financial reports;
- approving the annual accounts where applicable;
- deciding upon the dissolution of the Association;
- deliberating on matters submitted by the Board.

Unless otherwise provided by these Bylaws or by applicable law, decisions are adopted by a simple majority of the members present or represented.

Each member may hold no more than one proxy unless otherwise decided by the General Assembly.

---

## Article 7 — Board of Directors

The Association is administered by a Board.

The Association is administered by a President acting as the sole member of the Board until further appointment.

The Board may appoint additional officers, including a Treasurer, Secretary, Vice-President, or any other office necessary for the proper administration of the Association.

The Board is responsible for:

- implementing the decisions of the General Assembly;
- administering the activities and resources of the Association;
- preparing the annual budget and reports;
- ensuring compliance with applicable laws and these Bylaws.

The President is the legal representative of the Association and may delegate specific responsibilities in accordance with these Bylaws or by decision of the Board.

---

## Article 8 — Resources

The resources of the Association may include:

- membership fees;
- donations;
- grants;
- sponsorships;
- income generated by activities consistent with its purpose;
- public or private funding;
- any other lawful resources.

All resources shall be used exclusively to pursue the nonprofit purpose of the Association.

No part of the Association's net earnings may be distributed to its members.

---

## Article 9 — Amendment

These Bylaws may be amended by decision of the General Assembly in accordance with applicable French law.

Unless otherwise required by law, amendments shall be adopted by a simple majority of the members present or represented.

---

## Article 10 — Dissolution

The dissolution of the Association may only be decided by the General Assembly.

The General Assembly shall appoint one or more liquidators responsible for winding up the affairs of the Association.

After payment of all liabilities, the remaining assets shall be transferred to one or more nonprofit organizations pursuing objectives similar to those of the Association, in accordance with the **Loi du 1er juillet 1901** and applicable French law.

No remaining assets may be distributed among the members.

---

## Article 11 — Transitional Provision

The appointment of a **Mandataire** for the legal formation of the Association is governed exclusively by the Founding Act.

The Mandataire is a temporary representative appointed solely to complete the legal and administrative formalities necessary for the incorporation and initial establishment of the Association.

The role of Mandataire does not constitute a permanent governance office under these Bylaws and automatically ceases upon completion of the delegated responsibilities unless otherwise expressly authorized by the Board in accordance with applicable law.

After incorporation, the Association may, where necessary, appoint or re-appoint a Mandataire on a case-by-case basis for specific administrative or legal operations in France. Such appointment shall always be temporary, limited in scope, and shall not create a permanent governance position within the Association.

---

## Article 12 — Final Provisions

These Bylaws are adopted by the founding members and shall enter into force upon the registration of the Association under the **Loi du 1er juillet 1901**.

They constitute the governing charter of Earth Bridge Alliance and shall remain binding until amended or replaced in accordance with these Bylaws and applicable French law.
